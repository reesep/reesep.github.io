I was raised in Billings, Montana for most of my life. My father was very drawn to computers when I was growing up and because of this, my family always had a couple of computers in the house when I was growing up.... shout out to Windows XP! Experience

In the past few years, I have gained valuable experience and skills through a variety of industry jobs in both government and academeia. To learn more about these positions, please continue reading!

Air Force

In the Summer of 2019, I had the privilege to work as an intern for the Air Force. I spent 12 weeks as a PCIP Intern working on Hill Force Base in Utah. I spent most of the summer working with a DevOps development team creating an updated and modernized calibration software that tests the working condition of a (unclassified) mobile ground radar system. I gained meaningful knowledge regarding software development lifecycles, software engineering and software testing

TechLink

TechLink, located in Bozeman Montana, guides businesses in the process of evaluating and licensing cutting-edge inventions and technologies from federals research and development labs. For about a year, I have worked as a software engineering intern in the TechLink Software Engineering and Analysis Laboratory (TSEAL). Our job is to support TechLink's technology transfer effort by developing automated testing solutions for Department of Defense enterprise software. I gained experience collaborating with other developers working with a wide variety of testing tools and libraries.


To learn more about TSEAL, check out this video!

Teaching Assistant and Smarty Cats Tutor

I have had the opportunity to be a teaching assistant for the lower division Python classes for four semesters now. My duties include grading assignments, holding a weekly lab section holding and office hours, and helping students understand the topics taught in lecture.


I view being a teaching assistant as a privilege. I always strive to go above and beyond to help and give my students the tools and resources they need to succeed. It is always my goal to push my students to become great problem solvers and cultivate excitement around some of the most fascinating ideas and core concepts in programming, and to support each and every student along the way. I've found that students have a much larger impact on us than we give them credit for. I have become a better teacher, communicator, and programmer after each semester. Being a teaching assistant has shown me that I have a passion for teaching and helping others, which is why I plan on pursuing a career in undergraduate education in the future.
