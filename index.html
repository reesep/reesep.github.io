I was raised in Billings, Montana for most of my life. My Father was very drawn to computers, and my family always had a couple in the house when I was growing up.... shoutout to Windows XP! This was my first real exposure to computers and is what sparked my interests in computers and caused me to enroll in programming classes in high school. To continue pursuing my interest in computers and programming, I went to Montana State University to start a degree in Computer Science during the fall semester of 2016.


Despite living in Montana for most of my life, I am not much of an outdoorsman or skier (shocker, I know). Outside of school, I enjoy spending time with my friends and family. I am a huge fantasy football nerd and spend countless hours setting my lineup, analyzing matchups, and researching stats and players. I am a die hard fan of the New England Patriots and the Indiana Jones Movies. I am also a big fan of The Bachelor and The Bachlorette TV shows! When I have time, I enjoy playing video games with my pals. For several years, I was the Program Director of one of the largest online educational programs to help people improve in one of my favorite video games, League of Legends.

Experience

In the past few years, I have gained valuable experience and skills through a variety of industry jobs in both government and acadmeia. To learn more about these positions, please continue reading!

Air Force

In the Summer of 2019, I had the priveldge to work as an intern for the Air Force. I spent 12 weeks as a PCIP Intern working on Hill Force Base in Utah. I spent most of the summer working with a DevOps development team creating an updated and modernized calibration software that tests the working condition of a (unclassified) mobile ground radar system. I gained meaningful knowledge regarding software devolopment lifecycles, software engineering and software testing

TechLink

TechLink, located in Bozeman Montana, guides businesses in the process of evaluating and licensing cutting-edge inventions and technologies from federals research and devolpment labs. For about a year, I have worked as a software engineering intern in the TechLink Software Engineering and Analysis Laboratory (TSEAL). Our job is to support TechLink's technology transfer effort by developing automated testing solutions for Department of Defense enterprise software. I gained expereience collarborating with other developers working with a wide variety of testing tools and libraries.
