Experience

In the past few years, I have gained valuable experience and skills through a variety of industry jobs in both government and academia. To learn more about these positions, please continue reading!

Hoplite Industries

At the beginning of June 2020, I started working as a software engineer for Hoplite Industries, a cyber risk solutions company in Bozeman, Montana. As a software engineer, I develop various tools to deliver cutting-edge cyber security solutions for computer, cloud, and network systems.

Air Force

In the Summer of 2019, I had the privilege to work as an intern for the Air Force. I spent 12 weeks as a PCIP Intern working on Hill Force Base in Utah. I spent most of the summer working with a DevOps development team creating an updated and modernized calibration software that tests the working condition of a (unclassified) mobile ground radar system. This opportunity gave me knowledge and hands-on experience with software development lifecycles, software engineering and software testing

TechLink

TechLink, located in Bozeman Montana, guides businesses in the process of evaluating and licensing cutting-edge inventions and technologies from federals research and development labs. For over a year, I worked as a software engineering intern in the TechLink Software Engineering and Analysis Laboratory (TSEAL). Our job is to support TechLink's technology transfer effort by developing automated testing solutions for Department of Defense enterprise software. I gained experience collaborating with other developers working with a wide variety of testing tools and libraries.


To learn more about TSEAL, check out this video!

Teaching

I am very passionate about teaching computer science to students of all diverse backgrounds. I have been a teaching assistant at Montana State University for over two years and I am having an absolute blast being one. I view being a teaching assistant as a privilege. I always strive to go above and beyond to help and give my students the tools and resources they need to succeed. It is always my goal to push my students to become great problem solvers and create excitement around some of the most fascinating ideas and core concepts in programming and computer science. I care very much about my students and their education. I make a great effort to support each and every student along the way, no matter their background. I've found that students have a much larger impact on us than we give them credit for. I have become a better teacher, communicator, and programmer after each semester working with students. Being a teaching assistant has shown me that I have a passion for teaching and helping others. It is my goal to teach computer science at a college level after graduate school.
