Background

Fantasy Football is a math-based game based on the real-life production of NFL players. At the beginning of the season, participants will draft their roster of current NFL players and the winner is determined by who's team scores more "fantasty points". Participants earn these virtual fantasy points based on how well their players perform in their real-life games. Participants draft players that play different positions on the football field, including quarterbacks (throwers), runningbacks (runners), and wide receivers (catchers).
The formulas for calculating total fantasy points varies by what position the players play.

Quarterbacks:

  • 1 point for every 25 passing yards
  • 4 points for throwing a touchdown
  • -2 points for throwing an interception

Runningbacks:
  • 1 point for every 10 rushing yards
  • 6 points for a touchdown
  • -2 points for every fumble

Wide Receiver:
  • 1 point for every reception
  • 1 point for every 10 receiving yards
  • 6 points for every touchdown

For example, quarterback Patrick Mahomes had 4031 passing yards, 26 touchdowns, and 5 interceptions in the 2019 season, which is a total of 255.24 fantasy points (161.24 points from passing yards, 104 points from touchdowns, and -10 points from interceptions)
