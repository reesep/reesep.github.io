About Me

My name is Reese Pearsall. I am an instructor at Montana State University in the Gianforte School of Computing where I primarily teach progamming and cybersecurity classes. I spent most of my life growing up in Billings, Montana before moving to Bozeman to start school. I did my undergraduate at Montana State University, followed by grad school at Montana State University. Under the supervision of Dr. Clemente Izurieta., I researched malware dection methods and wrote my theis, An Evaluation of Malware Detection and Categorization through Graph-Based Machine learning methods

Beyond academia, I've spent time working for TechLink, a DoD software licensing and transfer company, Hoplite Industries, a Bozeman-based cybersecurity solutions company, and the United States Air Force, where I worked on testing and calibration software for a ground radar system.

Teaching

Below are the classes that I most commonly teach at Montana State University. Click on one of them to go to course website for the most recent offering.

CSCI 127
Joy and Beauty of Data

Introduction to programming and problem solving with Python! Functions, conditionals, loops, lists, files, dictionaries, object-oriented programming, and data science modules

CSCI 232
Data Structures and Algorithms

Advanced data structures and programming techniques and their application. Topics include: trees, balanced trees, graphs, dictionaries, hash tables, heaps. Examines the efficiency and correctness of algorithms.

SOCI XXX
Cybercrime

Types of cybercrime, methods of attack, law enforcement response, and judicial cases.

CSCI 460
Operating Systems

Operating systems design. Processes, threads, concurrent programming, and scheduling. Memory, file, and I/O management. Security issues.

CSCI 466
Networks

Network communication of computer systems. Emphasis on the OSI model, network organization, and different protocols


CSCI 476
Computer Security

Introduction to computer security. Covers security issues in software design and development from technical, social and legal viewpoints. Topics include cryptography, security models, software security, authentication, authorization, and system security.

Research

I am generally interested in cybersecurity as a whole, but my more specific interests are:

  1. Malware Analysis and Detection
  2. Cybercrime
  3. Software Security and Quality Assurance
  4. Computer Science Education

Malware Detection and Malware Analysis

My research involves detecting and categorizing malware before a system is compromised. My thesis, An Analysis of Graph-Based Machine Learning Methods for Malware Dectetion and Categorization, uses graph-based machine learning to dinstinguish between benign and malicious programs. Converting a binary to a graph representation, such as a control flow graph, can capture the structure and features of malware, and can be used to train a machine learning model to detect malicious programs.

I am also interested in categorization of different malware types. It can be valueable to know the type of malware (ie, a virus, ransomware, keylogger, etc) to effectively respond and deal with a potentially compromised system.
